Mapping Racial Segregation in Metropolitan Detroit
African American Population by block group, 2000 and 2010 U.S. Census
Under 14%
Over 75%
Use the layer control icon in the top right corner to switch the basemap between 2000 and 2010 Census data. Click on cities in Wayne, Oakland, Macomb, Washtenaw, Monroe and St. Clair counties for African American demographics, median income and total population in 2010. Block group data was processed and the choropleth was created in QGIS, made into a Mapbox basemap and displayed using Leaflet. The cities file is an external geojson brought in from Github. This map was made for part of the final project in an advanced GIS course at The New School in 2016.
Mapping Industry in North America
Click on the orange polygons for basic information about industrial sites. Use the search bar to navigate to other cities. Currently, Southwest Detroit’s industrial areas, and the Baton Rouge-New Orleans corridor along the Mississippi River in Lousiana are available. The data is the result of original research. The shapefiles were created in QGIS and are hosted and displayed by Carto.
Mapping Preindustrial Conditions in Michigan
This map was inspired by “The Great Lakes in Ojibwe” by the Decolonial Atlas, and data from the 1816 Federal Survey of Michigan, which details pre-industrial land conditions and native plant life. The orange spaces represent pre-industrial swamps and wetlands.

The arterial roads of Gratiot Avenue, Grand River Road and Michigan Avenue began as trading routes and trails for Native Americans. The locations of these trails were determined by the swamps and wetlands, as can be seen in this map by anyone familiar with the basic geography of Detroit. I made this map as an exploration of a deeper past, one that goes beyond Augustus Woodward’s influence and European contact.
